This is a poem that I wrote in 2020, inspired by a painting of a woman staring out a window and my experience staring out the window in my Pittsburgh hotel room.

Woman Looking Out The Window

The raging storm of the night before
left a gift for the woman of the house.
Curtains fly free as they frame an open scenery
from which the tears of the sky entered the house,
scattered landings on the wooden floor inside.

The woman wakes up to this indoor scene
speechless in face of Mother Nature’s gift.
With heavy steps, she grabs a towel
to wipe Nature’s tears off the floor;
no complaints, just a tired mother cleaning up a mess.

The wind gently hits the window glass,
sending soothing whistling sounds to her ears,
bringing hints of the salty sea to her nose,
gently patting her face with a calming breeze.
The woman is lured to look out the window,
welcoming her is the view of Nature’s beauty after the storm.

Mother Nature’s apology to the woman
“Thank you for your understanding”
